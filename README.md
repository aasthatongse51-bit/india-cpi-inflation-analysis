# india-cpi-inflation-analysis
Analyzed India’s CPI inflation data using Excel to uncover key inflation drivers, evaluate category-wise contribution, and assess the impact of COVID-19 and global fuel price fluctuations through time-series and correlation analysis.

Project Overview-

This project analyzes Consumer Price Index (CPI) inflation trends in India using official government data. The goal is to uncover key drivers of inflation, understand category-wise contributions, and evaluate the impact of major economic events such as COVID-19 and global oil price fluctuations.

🎯 Problem Statement

As a data analyst working with inflation data, the objective was to:

Identify category-wise contribution to CPI
Analyze Year-on-Year inflation trends (since 2017)
Deep dive into food inflation trends
Study the impact of COVID-19 on inflation
Evaluate the relationship between oil prices and inflation

🗂️ Dataset Description

Source: Government of India CPI dataset

Data Type: Monthly CPI Index

Coverage:
Urban (CPI-U)
Rural (CPI-R)
General Index

Categories:
Food & Beverages
Housing
Fuel & Light
Transportation
Healthcare
Education, etc.

🛠️ Tools & Techniques Used

Microsoft Excel
Data Cleaning & Missing Value Imputation (Moving Average)
Pivot Tables
Time-Series Analysis
Correlation Analysis (CORREL)
Inflation Calculations (MoM & YoY)
Dashboard Creation

📈 Analysis & Key Insights

1️⃣ Category Contribution to CPI
Grouped sub-categories into broader buckets (Food, Energy, Transport, etc.)
Calculated contribution % for the latest month

Insights:
Food category contributes the highest to CPI
Essential categories dominate inflation impact
CPI heavily influenced by daily consumption items

2️⃣ Year-on-Year Inflation Trend (Since 2017)
Calculated YoY inflation using CPI index
Visualized long-term inflation trend

Insights:
Peak inflation observed during 2021-2022
Inflation spikes linked to supply chain disruptions and global factors
Post-COVID period shows volatility in inflation

3️⃣ Food Inflation Deep Dive
Analyzed 12-month trend (ending May 2023)

Insights:
Highest food inflation observed in October 2022 (+1.01)
Lowest inflation in December 2022 (−1.35%)
Certain food categories contributed disproportionately

Key Finding:
Inflation spikes driven by essential commodities like cereals/vegetables

4️⃣ Impact of COVID-19 on Inflation
Compared inflation trends before and after March 2020

Insights:
Sharp increase in food and healthcare inflation post lockdown
Supply chain disruptions caused price volatility
Essential goods saw the highest inflation impact

5️⃣ Oil Prices vs Inflation (2021–2023)
Conducted correlation analysis between oil prices and CPI categories

Insights:
Strong correlation observed between oil prices and:
Transportation
Fuel & Energy
Indirect impact on other categories due to logistics costs
